## 📚 References & Literature Basis

STASis-Scan is grounded in peer-reviewed research. Below are the key studies that informed our model's design, feature selection, and calibration.

### Lung Cancer Epidemiology & Global Burden

1. **Sung H, et al. (2021).** Global Cancer Statistics 2020: GLOBOCAN Estimates of Incidence and Mortality Worldwide for 36 Cancers in 185 Countries. *CA: A Cancer Journal for Clinicians*, 71(3), 209-249. [DOI: 10.3322/caac.21660](https://doi.org/10.3322/caac.21660)
   - *Used for:* ~1.8 million annual lung cancer deaths worldwide; lung cancer as leading cause of cancer mortality

2. **Canadian Cancer Statistics Advisory Committee. (2024).** Canadian Cancer Statistics 2024. *Canadian Cancer Society*. [Available at: cancer.ca/Canadian-Cancer-Statistics-2024](https://cancer.ca/Canadian-Cancer-Statistics-2024)
   - *Used for:* 30,000+ annual diagnoses in Canada; 60% present with early-stage disease

### STAS Definition & Historical Context

3. **Travis WD, et al. (2015).** WHO Classification of Tumours of the Lung, Pleura, Thymus and Heart. 4th Edition. *IARC Press*.
   - *Used for:* Formal WHO recognition of STAS as distinct invasion pattern (2015)

4. **Kadota K, et al. (2015).** Tumor spread through air spaces is an important pattern of invasion and impacts recurrence after limited resection for small stage I lung adenocarcinomas. *Journal of Thoracic Oncology*, 10(5), 806-814. [DOI: 10.1097/JTO.0000000000000486](https://doi.org/10.1097/JTO.0000000000000486)
   - *Used for:* First large-scale STAS characterization; HR 2.32 for recurrence; STAS prevalence

5. **Travis WD, et al. (2024).** IASLC Staging Project: Recommendations for the 9th Edition TNM Classification. *Journal of Thoracic Oncology*. [DOI: 10.1016/j.jtho.2024.03.009](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jtho.2024.03.009)
   - *Used for:* STAS as histologic descriptor in upcoming TNM classification

### STAS Prevalence & Clinical Impact

6. **Huang L, Petersen RH. (2025).** Tumour spread through air spaces is a determiner for treatment of clinical stage I NSCLC: Thoracoscopic segmentectomy vs lobectomy. *Lung Cancer*, 201, 108438. [DOI: 10.1016/j.lungcan.2025.108438](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.lungcan.2025.108438)
   - *Used for:* STAS prevalence 19.2% in early-stage; HR 5.81 for STAS+ segmentectomy

7. **Shiono S, Yanagawa N. (2016).** Spread through air spaces is a predictive factor of recurrence and a prognostic factor in stage I lung adenocarcinoma. *Journal of Thoracic Oncology*, 11(3), 333-340. [DOI: 10.1016/j.jtho.2016.01.008](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jtho.2016.01.008)
   - *Used for:* STAS association with poor prognosis and recurrence

8. **Warth A. (2017).** Spread through air spaces (STAS) – a comprehensive update. *Der Pathologe*, 38(5), 371-378. [DOI: 10.1007/s00292-017-0349-6](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00292-017-0349-6)
   - *Used for:* Comprehensive STAS overview and clinicopathological characteristics

### Surgical Outcomes & STAS

9. **Lee J. (2025).** Spread through Air Spaces and Pleural Invasion in Sublobar Resection. *Journal of Chest Surgery*, 58(Suppl 1), S39. [DOI: 10.5090/jcs.2025s1.s9-5](https://doi.org/10.5090/jcs.2025s1.s9-5)
   - *Used for:* Meta-analysis of 15 studies (n=8,054); HR 2.58 for OS, HR 2.42 for RFS in STAS+ sublobar resection

10. **Eguchi T, et al. (2019).** Lobectomy is associated with better outcomes than sublobar resection in spread through air spaces (STAS)-positive T1 lung adenocarcinoma. *Journal of Thoracic Oncology*, 14(1), 87-98. [DOI: 10.1016/j.jtho.2018.09.005](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jtho.2018.09.005)
    - *Used for:* 5-year OS 73% (STAS+ sublobar) vs 87% (STAS-); first STAS-surgery interaction study

11. **Kagimoto A, et al. (2021).** Segmentectomy Versus Lobectomy for Clinical Stage IA Lung Adenocarcinoma With Spread Through Air Spaces. *Annals of Thoracic Surgery*, 112(3), 935-943. [DOI: 10.1016/j.athoracsur.2020.09.020](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.athoracsur.2020.09.020)
    - *Used for:* Lobectomy better for STAS+ patients

12. **Kadota K, et al. (2019).** Limited Resection Is Associated With a Higher Risk of Locoregional Recurrence than Lobectomy in Stage I Lung Adenocarcinoma With Tumor Spread Through Air Spaces. *American Journal of Surgical Pathology*, 43(8), 1037-1045. [DOI: 10.1097/PAS.0000000000001285](https://doi.org/10.1097/PAS.0000000000001285)
    - *Used for:* Limited resection risk in STAS+

13. **Ikeda T, et al. (2023).** Segmentectomy Provides Comparable Outcomes to Lobectomy for Stage IA Non–Small Cell Lung Cancer With Spread Through Air Spaces? *Seminars in Thoracic and Cardiovascular Surgery*, 35(2), 388-396. [DOI: 10.1053/j.semtcvs.2022.02.001](https://doi.org/10.1053/j.semtcvs.2022.02.001)
    - *Used for:* Nuanced analysis of segmentectomy in STAS+

### Molecular Characterization of STAS
